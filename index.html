MD-10-106 · Section 2.1–2.5
Mandatory Requirements
Baseline conditions all rail traffic must meet before entering the Queensland Rail network
2.1 — ALL RAIL TRAFFIC
Competent Worker in Charge
Rail traffic must have a Competent Worker — the Rail Traffic Driver or Competent Worker in Charge — who is responsible for the safe movement of rail traffic. The train must be operated safely and must not exceed the safeworking authority.
2.1 — ALL RAIL TRAFFIC
RTC / NCO Communication System
The Rail Traffic Crew / Network Control Officer communication system must be functioning correctly before the train enters the network.
2.3 — GENERAL (EXCL. CITYTRAIN UNITS)
Brake Pipe Continuity
The brake pipe (or equivalent system) must be continuous throughout the train.
2.3 — GENERAL
Main Reservoir Pressure
The compressed air main reservoir supply system must maintain pressure within the defined limits for the rollingstock.
2.3 — GENERAL
Inter-car Gangways
Inter-car gangways and canopies (if fitted) on passenger rollingstock must be secured.
2.3 — GENERAL
Event Recorder / JRU
If the event recorder or Juridical Recording Unit (JRU) is found faulty, the train may enter or continue in traffic but the condition must be reported before each entry into service and as soon as reasonably practicable after discovery.
2.3 — GENERAL
Handbrakes / Park Brakes
Handbrakes or park brakes must be functional and released except where specified.
2.3 — GENERAL
Brake System & Cab Instrumentation
The brake system, instrumentation (including instrumentation lighting) and cab lighting at the operating driving station must be functioning correctly.
2.3 — GENERAL
Draw Gear / Couplers
Draw gear/couplers must be coupled correctly and secure. All rail traffic must be correctly set up with hoses and cables correctly coupled.
2.3 — GENERAL
Defective Vehicle — RTD Obligations
If the RTD becomes aware that one or more vehicles may be defective, the crew must: (1) Stop if necessary and tell the NCO. (2) If required, protect the rail traffic. (3) Inspect for fault or failure, or arrange for inspection.
2.3 — GENERAL
Speed Indicator
Entering service: Must function at the operating driving station. En route: If defective or inaccurate, advise NCO. At RTD's discretion ensure permissible speeds not exceeded. Continue to: (a) re-marshal at first suitable location, (b) repair/replace, or (c) work out of service.
2.3 — GENERAL
Headlights
Entering service: Headlight must function on the leading driving cab. En route: If headlight fails, observe procedures in SG 8.6 of Observance of Signals Manual MD-10-109.
2.3 — GENERAL
Visibility Lights
Entering service: Visibility lights (where fitted) must function on the leading driving cab. En route: If failed on leading cab, treat as headlight only — refer SG8.4 and SG8.5 of MD-10-109.
2.3 — GENERAL
Whistles / Horns
Entering service: Must function on leading driving cab. En route: If failed, may continue at controlled speed to nearest depot or station for repairs. NCO must be told as soon as practicable.
2.3 — GENERAL
Marker Lights & Tail Lights
Entering service: Must function. En route: If defective, must be repaired or replaced as soon as possible — refer SG 8.3 of MD-10-109.
2.3 — GENERAL
Windscreens
Entering service: Leading windscreen must not impede RTD's vision. En route: If cracked/broken and posing a risk, may proceed at controlled speed to a repair location or re-marshal the unit away from lead.
2.5 — PASSENGER TRAIN UNITS
Automatic Train Brake System
The automatic train brake system must be functional — brakes must apply and release correctly at all times. A pre-departure test of the brake system must be carried out whenever a train is (a) marshalled for departure, (b) has an interruption to brake pipe continuity, or (c) parked unattended for more than 2 hours.

MD-10-106 · Section 2.6
Rail Traffic Protection Systems
Every train must have at least one signal system support AND one driver alertness support
🛡️ Required Combination
📡
Signal System Support
Must be one of: AWS (Automatic Warning System) or Station Protection
👁️
Driver Alertness Support
Must be one of: Vigilance or DSC (Driver Safety Control)
🔒 Systems Requiring Full Operability (Appendix 2)
🇪🇺
ETCS — European Train Control System
If fitted, must be operational in each driving cab
🚦
ATP — Automatic Train Protection
If fitted, must be operational in the leading driving cab
⚡ En Route — If System Fails
🔄
Exception: ATP Mode Available
If ATP is available in ATP Mode, the en-route failure rule does not apply
📍
If Signal System Support OR Driver Alertness Support Fails En Route
RTD is permitted to proceed to the next station with suitable refuge facilities until a second route-competent person is available. Note: For AWS failure in the Brisbane Suburban Area (BSA), another RTC member may perform the duties of the second person.
MD-10-106 · Section 2.7
Defective DSC
Driver Safety Control failure — entering service vs in-service requirements
ENTERING SERVICE
The Driver Safety Control (DSC) must be in place and operating before the train enters service. A defective DSC is a barrier to entering the network.
IN SERVICE
If the DSC becomes defective in service: (1) The Train Unit must terminate at the next station. (2) The train must then travel as a non-revenue service to a maintenance facility.
ℹ

Refer also to the full Driver Safety Control row in Operating Conditions (Appendix 2) for the detailed in-service procedure including Guard travelling in the driving cab.
